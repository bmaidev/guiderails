# Easy Read

**Status: DRAFT — NOT FOR PUBLICATION. 10 July 2026.**

## What is here, and what is not

`EOI-easy-read.md` is **drafted text**, written by the same people who wrote the plain-language original. That is not Easy Read.

Easy Read is a produced format with conventions this repository cannot satisfy on its own: one idea per line, short familiar words, a supporting image beside each idea, generous white space, a consistent layout, and — the part no drafter can substitute for — being **tested with people with intellectual disability**, who tell you what does not work.

Publishing drafted text as though it were Easy Read would be the project doing to Easy Read precisely what it accuses services of doing to accessibility: declaring conformance it has not earned. Criterion 2.1.1 exists because of that habit. So does gate C3.

## What this text is for

It exists so that the disability-led organisation engaged under D-018 has something to work from and to disagree with, and so that the cost and time of proper Easy Read production is visible in the plan rather than discovered late.

## Before anything here is published

1. **Produced** by people who do Easy Read work, paid at commercial rates. Images sourced or drawn; layout applied.
2. **Tested** with people with intellectual disability, who are paid for that testing.
3. **Consented** under gate **C3** — by LEDA, once LEDA exists. The exemption at D-018 covers the *establishment* pack only, and it ends when the panel is constituted. Everything after that goes through C3 normally.

The order matters. Producing after consent would present the panel with a fait accompli; consenting after production wastes the producer's work if the panel says no. The framework's answer is that C3 consent is sought on the produced artefact, and the panel is told plainly that saying no means paying for the work again. That cost is the project's, not the panel's.

## The one exception, and its limit

The Easy Read pair for the expression of interest is drafted *before* LEDA exists, because it is how LEDA comes to exist. Its production and review are done by a paid disability-led organisation acting at arms length from the steward. That substitute is **weaker** than LEDA consent and is described as weaker everywhere it appears. It is available once, for four documents, and never again.
