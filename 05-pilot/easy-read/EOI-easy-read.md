## About us

We are making a new rule book.

The rule book is about computer helpers.

A computer helper is a program that can use a website for you.
Some people call them AI agents.

Computer helpers can fill in forms.
They can apply for a payment for you.

This can help a lot of people.

It can also go wrong.
A computer helper might make a mistake.
It might agree to something you did not want.

There is no rule book to keep people safe.
We are writing one.

---

## We want you to check our work

We want 6 to 10 people with disability to join a group.

The group is called the Lived Experience Design Authority.

The group can say **no** to us.

When the group says no, we must stop.

---

## What the group says yes or no to

The group says yes or no to 4 things.

**1.** When we want to say this work helps people with disability.
We cannot say it until the group says yes.

**2.** When we test computer helpers.
The group says if our tests are like real life.
We cannot share our test results until the group says yes.

**3.** Our Easy Read documents.
The group says if they are good enough.

**4.** The rules for the group itself.

---

## The group also gives advice

The group gives us advice about the rule book.

We listen.
Then we decide.

If the group does not agree with what we decide,
we write down what the group said.
We use the group's own words.
We share it with our decision.

---

## The first thing we will ask you

We wrote the rules for the group before the group existed.

That is not a good way to do it.
We are telling you because it is true.

So the first thing we will ask the group is:

**Do you agree with these rules?**

You can change the rules.
You can say no to the rules.

If you say no, we write new rules.
You tell us what they should say.

---

## You get paid

You get paid for your time.

You get paid the same as other experts we pay.

You get paid for reading before the meeting.

You can bring a support person.
Your support person gets paid too.

You still get paid if you say no to us.

---

## How much time

About 1 meeting every 6 weeks.

Each meeting is about 2 hours.

You also read things before the meeting.

---

## You do not need to know about computers

You do not need to know about AI.

You do not need to know about computers.

You need to know what it is like to use these services.

---

## What help can you get

Tell us what you need.

You can have:
- an Auslan interpreter
- captions
- papers early
- papers in your way of reading
- shorter meetings
- more breaks
- a support person
- to write instead of talk

You do not have to explain why you need it.

You do not have to tell us about your disability to get help.

---

## Your private information

We keep your information private.

We do not put it on the internet.

We never write about your disability on the internet.
