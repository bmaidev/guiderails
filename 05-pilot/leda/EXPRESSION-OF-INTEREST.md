## We are looking for 6 to 10 people with disability to hold this project to account

AI agents are starting to use government services on people's behalf. For many people with disability, that could be the difference between getting a payment and giving up on it. It could also go badly wrong: an agent that misreads a rule, or acts without asking, commits *you* to something you never agreed to.

There is no standard for whether a government service is safe to use this way. We are writing one, and we are testing whether it is true.

We want a panel of people with disability who can stop us when we get it wrong.

## This panel has power, not a seat at the table

The Lived Experience Design Authority decides — not advises — on four things:

- whether we may publish any claim that this work benefits people with disability;
- whether the tasks we test agents against are realistic, before we score any results;
- whether our Easy Read and plain-language documents are good enough;
- whether the rules that govern the panel itself may change.

If the panel says no, the work does not proceed. We cannot publish over a refusal.

On everything else — how the standard is worded, what it demands, how strict it is — the panel advises, and where the panel disagrees with us, **we publish the disagreement in the panel's own words**.

## The first thing we will ask you to do is judge us

The framework that gives this panel its power was written before the panel existed. That is not ideal, and we are not going to pretend otherwise.

So the first substantive item at the first meeting is whether the panel accepts that framework. You can change it. You can reject it. If you reject it, we rewrite it the way you tell us to, before we use any of it.

## You are paid

Members are paid as co-designers, at a rate no lower than any other expert adviser to this project. If you take part with a supporter of your choosing, your supporter is paid for their time too. We publish whether we paid people on time, every phase.

## What it involves

About one two-hour meeting every six weeks, plus reading. Sometimes an extra session when a decision falls due.

You do not need to know anything about AI, standards, or computers. You need to know what these services are like to use.

## Who we are hoping to hear from

We are looking for a panel that spans intellectual disability, psychosocial disability, blind and low vision, Deaf and hard of hearing, physical and motor disability, and chronic illness and fatigue conditions. We want at least one First Nations member, members from culturally and linguistically diverse communities, and members outside the capital cities. We want people who have dealt with services that make you report, prove, and prove again. And we want at least one person who uses assistive technology daily, or teaches others to.

Nobody is asked to speak for a whole group.

## Access

Tell us what you need to take part. Auslan interpreting, captioning, documents early and in your format, Easy Read, plain language, shorter sessions, more breaks, taking part in writing instead of live, a supporter with you.

You will not be asked to justify what you need. You will not be asked for a diagnosis to get it.

## How to register interest

*(Channel to be confirmed with the disability-led organisation engaged to review this pack — see D-018. It must include at least: a plain web form that works with a screen reader and a keyboard alone, a phone number with a person on the end, an email address, and the National Relay Service.)*

Tell us, in whatever form suits you — writing, voice, video, or through your supporter or advocacy organisation:

1. Why this matters to you.
2. Anything about using government services that you think we would get wrong without you.
3. What you need in order to take part.

You do not need to write it formally. There is no word limit and no wrong answer.

## What we do with what you tell us

Your information is held outside this project's public repository and is never published there. Nothing about your disability, your access needs, or your dealings with any service is recorded in it.

If you are not selected, we will tell you, and we will tell you how the panel was composed.
