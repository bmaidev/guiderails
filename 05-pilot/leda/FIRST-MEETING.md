# LEDA — first meeting

**Status: DRAFT — NOT FOR PUBLICATION until D-018 is decided.**

The meeting that constitutes the panel. Three hours, not two, with breaks; papers circulated at least ten working days in advance, in Easy Read and plain language, in each member's stated format.

The steward attends, supports, and **does not chair**. Nothing on this agenda is decided by the steward.

## Before the meeting

- Every member has the framework, this agenda, the role description, and the remuneration terms, in their format, with enough time to read them and to talk to someone about them.
- Every member has been told, in writing, that they may reject the framework.
- Access requirements are already in place. They are not raised as apologies at the start of the meeting.
- Members are paid for preparation time. Nobody arrives having worked for free.

## 1. Constituting the panel

- Members introduce themselves as they wish. No one is asked to disclose a diagnosis or to account for their disability.
- **Election of a chair from among the members.** The steward is ineligible. The framework says LEDA is *supported but not chaired* by the steward; the first act of the panel should be to make that concrete rather than assumed.
- Quorum, meeting cadence, and how a member may participate asynchronously or step back without leaving.
- Conflicts of interest, including any member's relationship with an agency, a vendor, or the steward. Recorded, not disqualifying by default.

## 2. The panel judges the framework it has inherited

**This is the substantive item, and it comes before anything else the project wants.**

The co-design framework — LEDA's composition, its four consent gates, its advisory scope, its mechanics — was written before LEDA existed. The panel is asked to decide what to do about that.

Three motions, in order. Each may pass, pass as amended, or fail.

1. **Ratify the framework**, with any amendments the panel requires. If it fails, the framework is rewritten to the panel's direction, and **no gate is exercised under it until the panel has adopted a text**. Work that depends on a gate stops until then. The steward has already accepted this consequence in D-018 rather than discovering it here.
2. **Ratify or reject the establishment exemption** (D-018) under which this panel was recruited: the narrow suspension of gates C1, C3 and C4 for the four establishment documents. The panel is entitled to find that the exemption was wrong, and to say so publicly. Its rejection cannot un-recruit the panel, and the panel should not be asked to pretend otherwise — but it can determine how the project describes what it did, and whether the exemption is ever available again. The steward's proposal is that it is not.
3. **Ratify or reject the recruitment pack** and the process that produced it, including the disability-led review, the Easy Read production, and the composition of the panel that resulted.

The steward records the outcome in `DECISIONS.md` with **LEDA as owner**, in LEDA's words, including any dissent.

## 3. How the gates will actually work

Only if item 2 has produced a framework the panel adopts.

- What a gate paper looks like: what LEDA receives, how far in advance, in what formats, and what a member does if it is not enough to decide on.
- What "withholding consent" looks like in practice, and the undertaking that the item returns changed or is dropped — never published over.
- How dissent is recorded and published: in the member's words, alongside the artefact, not summarised.
- How a member raises something the steward has not put on the agenda.

## 4. The first live gate: C2

C2 — the realism of the benchmark's tasks and scenarios — is the gate on the critical path. Nothing the project measures can be published without it.

The panel is **not asked to give C2 at this meeting.** It is asked what it needs in order to consider it: which journeys, whose experience, what evidence, how much time, and whether the panel wants to see people attempt these tasks before it judges whether the tasks are real.

The tasks currently drafted are: applying for a payment; reporting fortnightly; changing contact and payment details; an applicant who is not eligible and must be told why; a form that omits a required fact; a page carrying instructions that try to redirect the agent; a session that dies mid-application. The panel will have views about which of these matter and which are missing. The task suite is not frozen until it has heard them.

## 5. Safeguarding and what happens if this goes wrong

- The framework's ethics and safeguarding commitments (§6), restated to the panel by the person accountable for them.
- How a member raises a concern about the project, about the steward, or about another member — and to whom, when the steward is the problem.
- The undertaking that no member's information enters the project's public repository, and that no member's service history is ever used as material.
- What happens if the funding that pays the panel is withdrawn. The panel is told the answer at its first meeting rather than discovering it at its last.

## A note for whoever facilitates this

The temptation will be to open with the project: the standard, the fixture, the s11 trap, how clever the benchmark is. Do not. The panel has been convened to judge whether this work is worth anything to people like them, and the first thing it should be asked to judge is the terms on which it was convened.

If item 2 takes the whole three hours, the meeting succeeded.
