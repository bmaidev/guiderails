# Independent review of the establishment pack

**Status: NOT COMPLETE. No reviewer engaged. 10 July 2026.**

D-018 is *Decided*. That suspended consent gates C1, C3 and C4 for this pack — **once, and never again**. It did not authorise publication.

Publication is authorised by this record, signed. Until then, every document in `05-pilot/leda/` and `05-pilot/easy-read/` carries the not-for-publication marking, and `07-governance/log-check` fails the build if any of them stops carrying it.

## What the reviewer is asked to do

The reviewer is a **disability-led organisation**, paid at commercial rates, independent of the steward, engaged for this purpose. Not a volunteer, not a favour, not an advisory chat.

They are asked to do four things, and **they may require changes to any of them before signing**:

1. **Read the pack as a prospective member would.** Role description, expression of interest, remuneration terms, first-meeting agenda. Is the authority real, as described? Is the ratification vote genuinely capable of failing? Would a person reading this understand what they are being asked to hold?
2. **Produce the Easy Read**, properly: written, illustrated, laid out, and **tested with people with intellectual disability who are paid for that testing**. What sits in `../easy-read/` today is drafted text and says so.
3. **Specify the registration channels.** The expression of interest deliberately leaves them blank. The reviewer decides what "accessible ways to reach us" actually means here, rather than the steward guessing.
4. **Say whether this should have happened at all.** The reviewer is invited to record, for publication, that recruiting a consent-holding body without its consent was the wrong way round — if that is their view. That statement is published with the pack, not filed.

## What this review is not

It is **weaker than LEDA consent** and must be described as weaker wherever it appears. A paid reviewer is not a body of 6–10 people with a veto. Nothing here substitutes for gate C1, C3 or C4 in the ordinary case; the substitution is confined to the establishment pack and expires when LEDA is constituted.

The reviewer is not asked to approve the standard, the benchmark, or the project. Only the pack, and only the process that produced it.

## After signing

1. Remove the not-for-publication markings from the pack, in the same commit that records this sign-off, and no earlier.
2. Approach partner organisations with a funded role.
3. Recruit against the composition targets in the framework §4.
4. Hold the first meeting. LEDA ratifies, amends, or rejects — including this exemption, and including the framework itself.

If LEDA rejects the framework at step 4, the framework is rewritten to LEDA's direction before any gate is exercised under it. Gate C2 stops with the rest, and no benchmark result is published until LEDA has adopted a text. The steward accepted that consequence in D-018 before recruiting anybody.
