# LEDA member — role description

**Status: DRAFT — NOT FOR PUBLICATION. D-018 is Decided; the independent review it requires is not. See [`REVIEW-RECORD.md`](REVIEW-RECORD.md).**

## The short version

You would sit on a panel of 6–10 people with disability that holds a **veto** over four things this project wants to do. Not an advisory group. A body whose refusal stops the work.

## What the project is

Government services are increasingly used by AI agents acting on a person's behalf — often by people for whom filling in a form, holding a phone queue, or reading a PDF is the barrier that stops them getting what they are entitled to. There is no standard for whether a government service can be used *safely* by such an agent. This project is building one, together with a way of testing whether the standard is true rather than merely written.

The project treats agents as assistive technology. That framing is a choice, and LEDA is where it gets tested against people who use assistive technology.

## What you would decide

Four **consent gates**. The work does not proceed without LEDA's consent, and the steward must not publish over a withheld gate.

| | |
|---|---|
| **C1** | Every public claim that this work benefits people with disability, before it is published. |
| **C2** | Whether the benchmark's tasks and scenarios are realistic, before any results are scored for publication. |
| **C3** | Easy Read and plain-language versions of the standard's public artefacts. |
| **C4** | Changes to the co-design framework itself. |

Consent needs a majority of members present, with at least three present. Where LEDA withholds consent, the item comes back changed or is dropped.

## What you would advise on

The wording of success criteria and how strict each one is; which parts of the standard matter most; how the pilot is designed; how the reference implementation behaves. Here the steward decides — and where LEDA disagrees, **the disagreement is published alongside the decision**, in LEDA's words.

## What we will ask of you first

The first meeting asks LEDA to decide whether to **ratify the framework it has inherited** — the document that defines LEDA's own powers, composition and gates. You may amend it. You may reject it. If you reject it, it is rewritten to your direction before any gate is exercised under it.

You are being told this before you apply, because a ratification that cannot fail is not a ratification. The framework was written without you. That is the honest starting position, and the first thing you are asked to do is judge it.

## Time, and what it is worth

Roughly one meeting every six weeks, two hours, plus reading time. Occasional additional sessions when a gate falls due.

**You are paid.** Members are remunerated as co-designers, not consulted as a checkbox — at a rate no lower than any other expert adviser to this project. If you participate with a supporter of your choosing, **your supporter is paid for their session time too**. Payment terms and the payment-integrity measure the project publishes each phase are in [`REMUNERATION.md`](REMUNERATION.md).

## Who we are looking for

Panel-level targets, not per-person quotas — nobody is asked to represent a category:

- Cross-disability: intellectual disability, psychosocial disability, blind and low vision, Deaf and hard of hearing, physical and motor disability, chronic illness and fatigue conditions.
- At least one First Nations member. CALD representation. Regional and remote representation.
- Current or recent lived experience of high-compliance service systems — the ones that require you to report, prove, and re-prove.
- At least one assistive-technology power user or trainer.

You do not need to know anything about AI, standards, or software. The panel needs people who know what it is like to use these services, and what happens when they fail.

## Access and support

Access requirements are met as a matter of course and are not a favour: Auslan interpreting, live captioning, materials in advance and in your preferred format, plain language and Easy Read, flexible session length, breaks, asynchronous participation where a meeting does not suit, and a supporter of your choosing.

Tell us what you need. You will not be asked to justify it, and you will not be asked to disclose a diagnosis to receive it.

## What we owe you

- Your dissent is published in your words, not summarised into agreement.
- Your consent is asked *before* the thing happens, not after it is built.
- Your information stays out of the project's public repository. Nothing about your disability, your access needs, or your service history is recorded there — ever.
- If the project cannot honour a gate, it says so publicly rather than proceeding quietly.

## What this role is not

It is not a reference group whose minutes are noted. It is not a lived-experience story to be used in a funding application. It is not a body that can be removed without the work stopping — that property is deliberate, and it is written into the framework so that removing LEDA visibly breaks the path to publication.
