# Remuneration

**Status: DRAFT — NOT FOR PUBLICATION. D-018 is Decided; the independent review it requires is not. See [`REVIEW-RECORD.md`](REVIEW-RECORD.md) The rate remains a steward decision and is deliberately not set here.**

## The principle

The framework says members are remunerated as co-designers, not consulted as a checkbox. That sentence has to survive contact with a budget, so this document states what the rate must satisfy rather than what it is. Naming a number here, in a repository the steward has not approved, would be the drafter setting the price of other people's expertise.

## What the rate must satisfy

1. **No lower than any other expert adviser to this project.** If the project pays a lawyer, an auditor, an adjudicator, or a technical consultant for an hour of expertise, a LEDA member's hour is worth no less. Lived experience is the expertise this panel is convened for.
2. **Paid for reading, not only for meeting.** Preparation is the work. A rate that covers only the two hours in the room pays for attendance and takes the analysis for free.
3. **Supporters paid for session time.** A member who participates with a supporter of their choosing does not fund that support out of their own fee (framework §4).
4. **Paid whether or not the member speaks, agrees, or attends the full session.** Payment is not contingent on giving consent, on giving it quickly, or on giving it at all. A payment a member could lose by refusing consent is a payment that buys consent.
5. **No unpaid probation, trial session, or "introductory" meeting.** The first meeting is work, and the first meeting is where the panel is asked to judge the framework.
6. **Benchmarked against a published external basis**, named in the decision that sets it, so a reader can check it. Candidate bases exist in the disability sector's own guidance on paying people with lived experience; the steward should adopt one by name rather than invent a figure.

## Practical terms

- **Payment within the stated terms**, and the framework §7 already commits the project to publishing, each phase, whether it paid on time and what the exceptions were. That measure exists because late payment is the most common way a co-design commitment quietly fails.
- **No requirement to hold an ABN, invoice, or company** in order to be paid. Where a member is on an income-support payment, the project provides, in writing and before engagement, a plain explanation of how payment may interact with their entitlements, and pays for independent advice if the member wants it. This is not optional: a co-design payment that costs a member their payment is a harm the project inflicted.
- **Costs are not the fee.** Travel, care, interpreting, captioning, assistive technology, and data are met by the project as costs of running an accessible process, and are not deducted from anyone's remuneration.
- **Nobody is paid in exposure, experience, or acknowledgement.** Acknowledgement is offered in addition, at the member's option and in the form they choose, including not at all.

## What the steward must decide (D-018 or a companion decision)

- The hourly or sessional rate, and the external basis it is benchmarked to.
- The preparation-time allowance per meeting.
- The supporter rate.
- Payment terms in days, and who is accountable for meeting them.
- The budget line, and what happens to the panel if that line is cut. **If the answer is that the panel stops, say so in advance**, because a body whose authority depends on continued funding by the party it constrains has less authority than the framework claims for it.

That last point is the uncomfortable one, and it belongs in the open rather than in a footnote discovered later.
